BEFORE

1. A 2-minute interview refuses vagueness

The prompt does not accept the fuzzy version. It keeps asking until the work is small enough to start and clear enough to finish.

THE NARROWING

User"Work on my book."

AI"A book is a project, not a place to start. Which scene?"

User"Chapter 9, the opening. I keep stalling on it."

AI"Four jobs, then: reread the last page, draft the scene start to finish — ugly allowed — mark the weakest paragraph, write one line for tomorrow. Edit that."

User"One page of rereading, not more. I'll spiral."

AI"Done — one page."

THE STAKES

AI"When do you sit down?"

User"9:00. Out by 10:30."

AI"Why does this matter today? Your words come back when you drift."

User"This book only gets real on the days I don't feel like it — and one drafted scene is the book actually happening."

AI"Kept, word for word. Last one — 10:30, scene drafted. How does that feel?"

User"Relief. The book moving again."

AI"Building your Focus File."

DURING

2. The Focus File holds the block

The doing happens here. A small offline dashboard holds the jobs, the reason, the start time, and the watch-for trap while you work.

TASK

Draft the Chapter 9 opening scene

WHY THIS MATTERS

"This book only gets real on the days I don't feel like it — and one drafted scene is the book actually happening."

ACTION LIST

  • ☐Reread the last pageOne page only — enough to find your voice again, not enough to spiral.
  • ☐Draft the scene to its endUgly allowed. A finished bad draft beats a polished fragment.
  • ☐Mark the weakest paragraphMark it, don't fix it — editing is not today's job.
  • ☐Write tomorrow's first lineOne sentence, so tomorrow starts moving, not deciding.

WATCH FOR

Opening the outline file and reorganizing notes instead of drafting.
