BEFORE

2-minute interview

It keeps asking until the work is small enough to start and clear enough to finish — then it collects the stakes, in your own words.

THE NARROWING

User"Work on my book."

AI"A book is a project, not a block. Which scene?"

User"Chapter 9. The opening. I keep rereading instead of writing."

AI"What would count as done by 10:30?"

User"A rough full pass. Around 1,500 words."

AI"I'd structure that as four jobs: reread only enough to restart, draft the scene ugly, mark the weakest paragraph, and leave one line for tomorrow. Edit that."

User"One page of rereading, not more — I'll stall."

AI"Locked: one page, then 1,500 rough words."

THE STAKES

AI"When do you sit down?"

User"9:00. Finish by 10:30."

AI"Why does this matter today?"

User"This book only gets real on the days I don't feel like it — and one drafted scene is the book actually happening."

AI"Kept, word for word. 10:30, 1,500 words down — how will that feel?"

User"Relief. The book moving again."

AI"Building your Focus File."

DURING

The Focus File

The doing happens here. A small offline dashboard holds the jobs, start time, and the words it will return if you drift.

TASK

Draft 1,500 rough words of the Chapter 9 opening

ACTION LIST

  • ☐Reread the last pageOne page only — enough to find your voice again, not enough to spiral.
  • ☐Draft 1,500 rough wordsUgly allowed. A finished bad draft beats a polished fragment.
  • ☐Mark the weakest paragraphMark it, don't fix it — editing is not today's job.
  • ☐Write tomorrow's first lineOne sentence, so tomorrow starts moving, not deciding.
